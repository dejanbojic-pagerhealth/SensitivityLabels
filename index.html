Public Approved for sharing with anyone inside or outside Pager Health ⌄
Public

Use this label for content that has been deliberately prepared and approved for public distribution. There is no confidentiality concern — sharing this content externally causes no harm to Pager Health.

Typical examples

Press releases Public website content Published job postings Marketing materials Public product documentation
Internal Day-to-day Pager Health content — keep within the organisation ⌄
Internal

Use this label for general business content intended for use within Pager Health. Sharing it externally could create a confidentiality risk, but the content does not include sensitive personal data or business-critical information. This is the default label applied to new documents.

Typical examples

Internal memos Team meeting notes Internal process documents Project plans Internal training materials Org charts
Confidential Sensitive content — a breach would cause harm to Pager Health ⌄
Confidential

Use this label for content specifically intended for internal employees or approved external parties. A breach of confidentiality would cause meaningful harm to Pager Health, its employees, or its partners. This includes personal information, financial data, legal documents, and other sensitive business content that should not be shared outside the organisation without approval.

Typical examples

Employee personal records Contracts and legal agreements Financial reports Partner or vendor agreements Internal audit findings Salary and compensation data
Restricted Highest sensitivity — a breach would cause serious harm ⌄
Restricted

Use this label for the most sensitive information at Pager Health. A breach would cause serious harm — legal, regulatory, or reputational. This includes protected health information (PHI) under HIPAA, credentials, and strategic business data. Choose the sub-label that best describes the content type.

Sub-labels

Restricted / PHI
Protected health information covered by HIPAA — patient records, clinical notes, treatment data, or any information that identifies an individual in a health context.
Restricted / Credentials
Passwords, API keys, certificates, access tokens, or any authentication material. Never store credentials in documents — use a password manager or secrets vault.

Which label should I use?

Answer a few questions to get a recommendation.

1. Does the content include protected health information (PHI), credentials, or strategic data whose exposure would cause serious harm to Pager Health?

2. Does the content include personal information, financial data, or details shared with specific named parties under a confidentiality expectation?

3. Has this content been reviewed and approved for release outside Pager Health?

Recommended label

Restricted

Select the appropriate sub-label: Restricted / PHI for health information, Restricted / Credentials for passwords or keys.

Recommended label

Confidential

Changing a label to a lower classification

When you apply a label with a lower classification than the current one — for example, changing a document from Confidential to Internal — Pager Health requires a justification. A dialog will appear asking you to select one of the following options.
Previous label no longer applies
The content has changed or its context has shifted so that the original label is no longer appropriate. For example, a document that contained draft financial projections has been revised to remove all sensitive figures.
Previous label was incorrect
The original label was applied in error — the content never required that level of classification. Use this when correcting a labeling mistake rather than responding to a content change.
Other (explain) — Do not enter sensitive information
Neither of the above options fits. You will be asked to type a brief explanation in your own words. Do not include personal data, credentials, or any sensitive content in this field — it is recorded in the audit log and visible to the Information Security team.
⚠
All label changes are recorded in the Microsoft Purview audit log. If you are unsure whether a downgrade is appropriate, contact the Information Security team before making the change.
